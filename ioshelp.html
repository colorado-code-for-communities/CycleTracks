Open Bike Instructions

Upon launching the app, you’ll find yourself on the “Record” screen.

Confirm the iPhone has a good fix on your location by checking the map before tapping Start. (GPS conditions sometimes prevent accurate location. If this is the case, cycle a short distance and check again.)

When you're ready to start recording your ride, tap “Start.”

 

 
     

1. Recording a ride

Open Bike will begin recording. At this point, you can background the Open Bike application (by pushing the home button) if you want to use other applications on your phone. The app will vibrate and ring like a bicycle bell periodically to remind you it is recording. If your battery goes below a 20% charge, the app will automatically stop recording and shut down.
     
 

2. Pausing recording

To pause recording (for example if you stop for a few minutes for coffee), you should force quit the app: double press the “Home” button when the app is in background mode to reveal the running apps along the bottom of the screen below the dock. Find the Open Bike app icon and tap and hold the icon until a red badge with a minus appears. Tap the minus sign to force quit the app. When you open it again, you’ll be notified that the recording was interrupted, and given the option to resume or cancel recording.

     
 

3. Specifying the trip purpose

Select a purpose on the “Trip Purpose” screen and tap “Save.” We’re interested in the MAIN purpose for each trip you make. If you’re unsure how to categorize a trip, select “Other” from the Trip Purpose chooser. You can always include a comment before transmitting.

Open Bike will prompt you to enter comments or notes on the ride, which you can skip if you have none. It will then upload your ride data, notifying you when done. If there’s a problem connecting to the server, it will prompt you to retry at another time.

 
     
 

4. Maps

After your data is uploaded, Open Bike displays the map of your ride, showing the route, distance travelled, and average speed.

"My Trips" in the tab-bar at the bottom of the screen shows a list of all your rides, with dates, times, and mileage. Tap on a trip to display its map. Share the map with others by zooming in or out, taking a screenshot (press Sleep/wake and Home simultaneously), and sending it via email or downloading it to your computer.

Tap “Edit” on the trip list to delete a trip. If Open Bike finds any trips recorded with the first version of the app that show a zero mileage, it will notify you and recalculate the distance.
     
 

5. Settings

Entering personal info is completely optional and confidential, and, if you choose to participate, only has to be done once. It involves a few basic questions useful to our study. If you wish to give us your email address (which will not be shared with any third parties), we can keep you updated on the progress of the study. Tap “Save” when finished.
